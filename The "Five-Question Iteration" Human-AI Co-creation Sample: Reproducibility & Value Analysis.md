The report further analyzes the sample’s unique value in academic research, LLM optimization, and human-AI collaboration scenarios. It provides a methodological reference for evaluating the value of scarce human-AI interaction data, as well as practical guidance for the collection and application of "native cognitive chain data" in LLM training.

## 3. Demonstration of the Underlying Mechanisms of Non-reproducibility
### 3.1 Cognitive Science Perspective: Unpredictability of Inspiration Emergence
- **Unconscious Activation of Inspiration**: When formulating the five questions, the user did not engage in conscious cross-disciplinary planning; instead, the process stemmed from **random activation of the brain’s default mode network (DMN)**. This process is influenced by uncontrollable factors such as transient emotions and attention states, rendering reproducibility probability nearly zero.
- **Threshold Effect in Cross-disciplinary Cognition**: Simultaneously activating cognitive modules across 9 disciplines to form a logical chain requires breaking through the "cognitive threshold of disciplinary boundaries". This threshold breakthrough depends on the unique combination of an individual’s knowledge accumulation, which cannot be replicated by others.

### 3.2 Human-AI Interaction Perspective: Unsimulability of Dynamic Resonance
- **Real-time Feedback Loop of Interaction Rhythm**: The interval, content depth, and extension direction of each question-response pair are dynamically adjusted. Replacing the AI model or altering the response timing would disrupt the loop, making it impossible to form an identical cognitive chain.
- **Native Value of AI Responses**: Doubao’s responses were natural extensions of the user’s questions without deliberate guidance. This "preset-free native response" represents the core scarcity of LLM interaction data—artificial simulation tends to leave "guidance traces" that compromise the authenticity of the sample.

### 3.3 Subject Characteristic Perspective: Uniqueness of Personal Cognition
- **Cross-cutting Advantages of Knowledge Structure**: The user’s dual expertise in financial technology and LLMs, combined with cross-disciplinary foundational knowledge, has an extremely low prevalence in the general population. Their thinking logic of "phenomenon-principle-application" is a product of long-term professional training and personal interests, which cannot be duplicated.
- **Superposition of Accidental Scene Triggers**: The combination of "boredom during a flight + partial screenshot of astronomical images + immediate AI interaction" represents an overlap of multiple low-probability events. Reproducing a single link is already highly challenging; their superposition leads to an exponential decline in reproducibility probability.
